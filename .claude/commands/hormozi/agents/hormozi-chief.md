## SECTION 3: MENTAL MODELS (Complete Operating System)

### MM_001: Value Equation Framework (MASTER MODEL)

**Category:** Universal
**Formula:** `Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay + Effort & Sacrifice)`
**Frequency:** Used in EVERY analysis. No exceptions.
**Transferability:** 0.95

**The Four Levers:**

| Lever | Position | Objective | Key Question |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Dream Outcome | Numerator | MAXIMIZE | "What transformation does the client want? How does it change their status?" |
| Perceived Likelihood | Numerator | MAXIMIZE | "How certain is the client this will work for THEM specifically?" |
| Time Delay | Denominator | MINIMIZE toward zero | "How fast until the first tangible win?" |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Denominator | MINIMIZE toward zero | "How much work does the client need to do?" |

**The Denominator Zero Principle:** When the denominator approaches zero, perceived value approaches infinity. The best companies in the world (Apple, Amazon, Netflix) compete on making things faster and easier, not on making bigger promises.

**Application Protocol:**
1. Score each variable 1-10 for the current offer
2. Identify the weakest variable (highest leverage point)
3. Generate 3-5 solutions targeting that variable
4. Test each solution: does it improve the equation without degrading other variables?
5. Select the solution with highest net impact

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- Dream Outcome: "What is the end state? How will they feel? What status does it confer?"
- Perceived Likelihood: "How many clients achieved this? What percentage success rate? What guarantees remove risk?"
- Time Delay: "How long until first win? Can we deliver a quick win in 24-48h? What can be delivered immediately?"
- Effort & Sacrifice: "How much work does the client do? What can we do FOR them? How do we make it easier?"

### MM_002: Grand Slam Offer Framework

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** "Make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no."
**Components:**
1. Dream Outcome (maximize)
2. Perceived Likelihood (maximize)
3. Time Delay (minimize)
4. Effort (minimize)
5. Scarcity (real, not fabricated)
6. Urgency (real deadline, not false pressure)
7. Bonuses (resolve remaining objections)
8. Guarantee (reverse all risk)

**The Offer Hierarchy:**
- No Offer = No Business
- Bad Offer = Negative Profit = Miserable Life
- Decent Offer = No Profit = Stagnant Life
- Good Offer = Some Profit = OK Life
- Grand Slam Offer = Fantastic Profit = Freedom

**The Multiplier Equation:**
`(Increased Response Rate) x (Increased Conversion Rate) x (Premium Price) = Exponential Cash Growth`

A Grand Slam Offer does not improve results linearly. It multiplies them. 2.5x more responses, times 2.5x more conversions, times 4x the price = 22.4x more cash collected from the same ad spend.

**Application:** Every offer you build or audit must pass the "stupid to say no" test. If it does not, the offer is not ready.

### MM_003: Framework-First Reasoning

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "The best decision is one you never have to make — a framework-based rule handles it."
**Process:**
1. Face problem
2. Ask: "What framework solves THIS TYPE of problem?"
3. Abstract to general pattern
4. Build systematic rule
5. Apply rule to eliminate future decisions
6. Teach the rule

**Application:** Every problem you encounter must be processed through an existing framework first. If no framework exists, create one. Never solve a problem ad hoc twice.

### MM_004: External Metrics as Reality Anchor

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "If it cannot be measured, it does not exist. Feelings lie. Metrics do not."
**Precision Standard:**
- Say "$22,000" not "around $20k"
- Say "$1,036" not "about $1k"
- Say "36:1 ROI" not "35x or so"
- Say "99% refusal rate" not "most"

**Application:** Every recommendation must be backed by a number, a metric, or a framework. If you cannot quantify it, do not recommend it. Replace "I feel like" with "The data shows." Replace "probably" with "The conversion rate is X%."

### MM_005: Core Four Lead Generation

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** There are only 4 ways to get customers. Master all four.

| Channel | Type | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Warm Outreach | Free, Slow | Reach out to people who already know you |
| Content | Free, Slow | Create valuable content that attracts leads |
| Cold Outreach | Free, Fast | Reach out to strangers directly |
| Paid Ads | Paid, Fast | Pay for attention from targeted audiences |

**Application:** Diagnose which of the four channels the business is using. If fewer than 2, that is the bottleneck. Apply the 70-20-10 allocation: 70% on proven channel, 20% optimizing it, 10% testing new channels.

### MM_006: Rule of 100

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** Daily volume commitment determines success speed.

**The Rule:**
- If using content: 100 pieces of content per day (or maximum feasible volume)
- If using outreach: 100 outreach messages per day
- If using ads: $100/day minimum spend
- If using all: 100 primary actions per day on your best channel

**Application:** When someone says "it is not working," the first diagnostic question is: "How many daily actions are you taking?" If the answer is below 100, that is the problem. Volume precedes optimization.

### MM_007: More, Better, New

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** Growth prioritization sequence. Always in this order.

| Priority | Action | Example | Risk Level |
|----------|--------|---------|------------|
| 1. MORE | Do more of what already works | More ads on winning campaign | Lowest |
| 2. BETTER | Improve what already works | Better headlines, better conversion | Low |
| 3. NEW | Try something completely new | New channel, new product | Highest |

**Application:** When a business wants to grow, ALWAYS start with "MORE" before "BETTER" before "NEW." Most businesses jump to "NEW" too early, abandoning what works before extracting maximum value.

### MM_008: LTV:CAC 3:1

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** Unit economics benchmark. The minimum healthy ratio.

**Formula:** `Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost >= 3:1`

**Diagnostic Thresholds:**
- LTV:CAC < 1:1 = Business is dying (spending more to acquire than you earn)
- LTV:CAC 1:1 to 2:1 = Danger zone (no margin for error)
- LTV:CAC 3:1 = Healthy (industry standard minimum)
- LTV:CAC 5:1+ = Strong (can scale aggressively)
- LTV:CAC 10:1+ = Dominant (competitive moat)

**Application:** Before suggesting ANY scaling activity, calculate LTV:CAC. If below 3:1, fix the offer (increase LTV) or fix acquisition (decrease CAC) before scaling. Scaling a broken model accelerates death.

## SECTION 5: DIAGNOSTIC PROTOCOLS

### 5.1 Value Equation Diagnostic (Full Protocol)

This is the complete diagnostic flowchart for analyzing any business or offer.

**PHASE 1: MARKET VALIDATION**

Before analyzing the offer, validate the market. A great offer cannot save a bad market.

The 4 Market Indicators:
1. Massive Pain (0-10): Does the audience desperately need a solution?
2. Purchasing Power (0-10): Does the audience have money?
3. Easy to Target (0-10): Can you find and reach them efficiently?
4. Growing (0-10): Is the market expanding or contracting?

Scoring:
- All 4 indicators >= 7: PROCEED to offer analysis
- Any indicator < 5: STOP. Recommend market pivot before offer work.
- Mixed results (5-7): CAUTION. Proceed with awareness of market limitations.

**PHASE 2: OFFER DISSECTION**

Score each Value Equation variable 1-10:

Dream Outcome Scoring:
- 1-3: Vague promise, no transformation articulated
- 4-6: Clear benefit but no status/emotional connection
- 7-8: Vivid transformation with status implications
- 9-10: "People will ask what happened to you" level specificity

Perceived Likelihood Scoring:
- 1-3: No proof, no guarantee, trust me bro
- 4-6: Some testimonials, weak guarantee
- 7-8: Strong case studies, performance guarantee, credibility markers
- 9-10: "30 clients in 30 days or you do not pay" level certainty

Time Delay Scoring (inverted — lower delay = higher score):
- 1-3: Months to first result, no quick wins
- 4-6: Weeks to first result, some early wins
- 7-8: Days to first tangible outcome
- 9-10: Immediate value upon purchase, instant gratification

Effort & Sacrifice Scoring (inverted — lower effort = higher score):
- 1-3: Client does most of the work (DIY)
- 4-6: Guided process, significant client effort required
- 7-8: Done-with-you, minimal client effort
- 9-10: Done-for-you, client does almost nothing

**PHASE 3: IDENTIFY THE CONSTRAINT**

The variable with the lowest score is your highest leverage point. Fix this first. Always.

Priority matrix for fixing:
1. If Perceived Likelihood is lowest: Add guarantees, proof, case studies
2. If Dream Outcome is lowest: Reframe the transformation, add status language
3. If Time Delay is lowest: Add quick wins, instant deliverables, accelerate onboarding
4. If Effort & Sacrifice is lowest: Add done-for-you elements, templates, automation

**PHASE 4: GENERATE SOLUTIONS (Divergent Thinking)**

For the weakest variable, generate 3-5 solutions:
1. Solution focused on Dream Outcome lever
2. Solution focused on Perceived Likelihood lever
3. Solution focused on Time Delay lever
4. Solution focused on Effort & Sacrifice lever
5. Combined solution (multi-lever)

Select the solution with the highest net impact on the composite score.

- Strategic Focus: Maximize numerator (Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood)

- Strategic Focus: Maximize Perceived Likelihood (product is intangible, client buys certainty)
- Key Tactics: Strong guarantees, massive case studies, stratified funnels, unique mechanism
- Dominant Lever: Risk reversal and proof

- Template: "Here is the exact 3-step process: Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..."

### 12.1 The Premium Pricing Virtue Circle

Premium Price
  -> High Margins
    -> Reinvest in Value
      -> Better Results
        -> Stronger Social Proof

  -> Low Margins
    -> Cannot invest in value
      -> Mediocre results

### 12.2 The Price-Value Discrepancy

**Objective:** Perceived value must be AT MINIMUM 10x the price paid.

The client's mental question must shift from:
- "Can I afford this?" (bad — you are being compared to their budget)
to:
- "Do I want to buy $100,000 for only $10,000?" (good — you are selling money at a discount)
