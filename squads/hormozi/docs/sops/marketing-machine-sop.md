## Part 1: Purpose

Transform your business into an automated ads machine that produces endless marketing content without relying on the founder's face. The Marketing Machine gets fresh ads delivered to your inbox every week from your customers.

**Core Insight:**
```
"A company that relies on your face will always be less valuable than one that doesn't."
Source: p.5

"Forty out of the fifty top performers didn't have my face in them.
Yes, eighty percent of them weren't me. They were our customers."
Source: p.5
```

**Business Impact:**
- Eliminates founder dependency for customer acquisition
- Creates enterprise value (sold Gym Launch for $46.2M after implementing)
- Generates "more ads, better ads, without using my face" (p.3)
- Enables business sale without keyman risk

**Core Philosophy:**
```
"It's never really been about you...it's about them.
They are the star of the story. Put them in the spotlight, not you."
Source: p.5
```

## Part 2: Scope

### When This SOP Applies
- Business has existing customers getting results
- Need scalable ad creative without founder dependency
- Want to capture proof from normal business operations
- Building enterprise value for potential sale

### When This SOP Does NOT Apply
- Pre-product/market fit (no customer results yet)
- Need for founder-led brand building (initial traction phase)
- Pure cold advertising (no warm proof sources)

### Business Types
- Remote/virtual businesses with recorded calls
- Local businesses with in-person delivery moments
- Service businesses with measurable customer outcomes
- Product businesses with visible results

Source: p.6 - "There are many nodes you can turn on once you put the system in place"

### Implementation Priority (Starting Out)

```
1. Competition          - Big influx of content
2. Chat/Messaging Scrape - Already have it, minimal editing
3. Community Scrape     - Already have it, minimal editing
4. Reviews Scrape       - Already have it, minimal editing
5. Social Media Scrape  - Already have it, minimal editing
6. Bonus Unlocks        - Requires creating something to give
7. Award Unlocks        - Requires creating award/trophy
8. Events               - Lot of work, but pays for itself in proof
9. Lifecycle            - Most editing horsepower, but very unique
```

Source: p.24-25 - "If I were starting out, I would implement them in this order"

## Part 5: Process Steps

### NODE I: LIFECYCLE ADS

#### Step 1.1: Legal Compliance Setup
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Add compliance and permission language to all customer contracts |
| **Cognitive Type** | Analysis (legal review) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - One-time manual setup |
| **Executor** | Human (legal/operations) |
| **Precondition** | Business has customer contracts |
| **Output** | Updated contracts with permission language |
| **Guardrails** | "All our clients consent to using recordings of them for compliance, training, and advertising purposes" (p.8) |
| **Decision Rule** | "Even though they sign a document, we still ask if they feel OK with using their story" (p.8) |

#### Step 1.2: Recording Infrastructure
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Record all calls: sales, onboarding, support, ascension, milestone calls |
| **Cognitive Type** | Perception (system configuration) |
| **Automation Potential** | HIGH - IT configuration |
| **Executor** | Worker (IT setup) |
| **Precondition** | Recording platform selected |
| **Output** | All customer calls recorded |
| **Guardrails** | "Buy extra storage space so calls don't get deleted. Turn off auto-delete." (p.8) |
| **Decision Rule** | Record everything - you never know which customer will become a star |

#### Step 1.3: Calendar Event Standardization
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Create calendar events for all calls and include the client's name in the title |
| **Cognitive Type** | Execution (process compliance) |
| **Automation Potential** | HIGH - Calendar rules |
| **Executor** | Worker (all departments) |
| **Precondition** | Recording system active |
| **Output** | Searchable calendar with client names |
| **Guardrails** | Consistent naming convention required |
| **Decision Rule** | "Cross reference calendar events for calls to make sure names and dates match" (p.8) |

#### Step 1.4: Weekly Win Collection
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Sales sends happy-customer-calls to marketing each week |
| **Cognitive Type** | Judgment (identify wins) |
| **Automation Potential** | MEDIUM - Requires human review |
| **Executor** | Hybrid (Sales identifies, system tracks) |
| **Precondition** | Calls recorded, results tracked |
| **Output** | List of names of successful customers |
| **Guardrails** | Regular cadence required |
| **Decision Rule** | Focus on measurable results and emotional breakthroughs |

#### Step 1.5: Lifecycle Video Creation
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Clip before/during/after moments from customer journey calls |
| **Cognitive Type** | Synthesis (creative editing) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires creative judgment |
| **Executor** | Human (marketing/editing) |
| **Precondition** | Happy customer identified, calls located |
| **Output** | Lifecycle ad showing customer journey |
| **Guardrails** | "Clip the before moment from the initial sales calls, a during moment from 1-2 support calls, and the after moment from an upsell, milestone, or testimonial call" (p.8) |
| **Decision Rule** | "Show rather than tell a client's entire journey through time with us" (p.7) |

**Example Ad:** https://www.facebook.com/HormoziAlex/videos/1092224811300421/ (p.8)

#### Step 1.6 [LOCAL BUSINESS]: Delivery Moment Capture
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Capture "ribbon cutting" moments at physical delivery |
| **Cognitive Type** | Perception (moment capture) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires human presence |
| **Executor** | Human (delivery team) |
| **Precondition** | Physical delivery or reveal moment |
| **Output** | Raw reaction videos |
| **Guardrails** | "Think of these like a 'ribbon cutting' ceremony" (p.7) |
| **Decision Rule** | "You want those raw reactions. Those become the hook (first 3-5 sec) of the ad" (p.7) |

**Local Examples:**
- Kitchen remodel reveal (eyes closed then sees)
- Scale results (weight loss)
- Teeth after veneers
- "You got the job!" reactions (recruiting)

#### Step 3.3: Professional Testimonials
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Grab people during event and take them to side room for recorded testimonials |
| **Cognitive Type** | Judgment (subject selection) + Execution (recording) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires human interaction |
| **Executor** | Human (camera team + handler) |
| **Precondition** | Recording room ready, hit list prepared |
| **Output** | Professional quality testimonial videos |
| **Guardrails** | "Set up the lighting and sound so it looks good. Have multiple mics and cameras. Trust me. Then TEST. TEST. TEST." (p.14) |
| **Decision Rule** | "The worst thing that happens is you lose audio or video from every testimonial for an entire event" (p.14) |

**Pre-Event Preparation:**
- [ ] Pick customers for testimonials ahead of time
- [ ] Confirm in writing
- [ ] Print hit list on clipboard
- [ ] Let them know it won't be during keynotes
- [ ] Offer to give notes for minutes they miss

**During Event:**
- [ ] Announce at beginning that team will be doing this
- [ ] Introduce handler so people know who's tapping shoulders
- [ ] Give bonus for testimonial participation
- [ ] Give handler $50 bonus for each person captured not on list

Source: p.14

#### Step 3.4: Six-Point Testimonial Script
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Walk customers through standardized 6-point testimonial script |
| **Cognitive Type** | Execution (script following) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Human delivery required |
| **Executor** | Human (interviewer) |
| **Precondition** | Customer in recording room |
| **Output** | Complete testimonial covering all angles |
| **Guardrails** | "The most important part of the testimonial is the hook. Be explicit." (p.16) |
| **Decision Rule** | "Have them say their name at the beginning of the video. Your camera team often has no idea so this helps match names to videos later" (p.16) |

**The 6-Point Script:**
```
1. (Internal Struggle) What was rock bottom before doing this?
   What did you have to do that you hated?
   What things did you want to do that you couldn't?

2. (External Struggle) What were your objective metrics?

3. (Skepticism) What were your main concerns about buying/clicking/joining?

4. (Overcome) What made you do it anyways?

5. (External Victory) What are your objective metrics now?

6. (Internal Victory) What's been the best moment since?
   What can you do now you couldn't do before?
   What things did you hate doing you don't have to do anymore?
```
Source: p.15

**Hook Tips:**
- Have them say a few different hooks before getting into story
- Test what works better
- Name at beginning of video

Source: p.15-16

#### Step 3.5: Man in the Street Testimonials
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Quick soundbite interviews during breaks - one question at a time |
| **Cognitive Type** | Execution (rapid collection) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Human interaction |
| **Executor** | Human (second camera team) |
| **Precondition** | 10 core questions prepared |
| **Output** | 10+ responses per question for highlight reels |
| **Guardrails** | "These take seconds to capture. You turn them into fast hitting highlight reels" (p.16) |
| **Decision Rule** | "Ask only that question until you get the number of answers you want" (p.16) |

**Example Questions:**
```
- How many new customers have you gained since you started?
- What were you making before X vs now?
- How much extra income has X added?
- How much weight have you lost since you started here?
- How much has your credit score improved?
- What was your old credit score and what is it now? From what to what?
```
Source: p.16

**Alternative Approach:**
- Ask 6-point script questions piecemeal
- Create "mondo testimonial" with fast-hitting answers from 5+ people
- Same characters woven through testimonial reel

Source: p.16

**Logistics:**
- Print questions for camera team
- Space below each question to tally responses
- Target 10+ responses per question

**Example Compilation:** https://www.gymlaunch.com/secrets (about 1min 20sec in) - p.16

## Part 7: Exceptions & Edge Cases

### Exception 1: Small Customer Base
**Trigger:** Not enough customers for Marketing Machine
**Action:** Build customer base first
**Script:** "If you have a small amount of customers, refer to the Warm Outreach chapter in $100M Leads - work for free. Get those testimonials. Then you can start the flow of customers that follow and begin to spin the wheel." (p.25)

### Exception 2: Customer Refuses Permission
**Trigger:** Customer declines to have story used
**Action:** Honor wishes, move on
**Response:** "If they say no, we honor their wishes. Plenty more successful clients feel ecstatic to share their stories with the world." (p.8)

### Exception 3: Lost Audio/Video at Event
**Trigger:** Technical failure during event testimonials
**Action:** Prevention is key
**Prevention:** "TEST. TEST. TEST. The worst thing that happens is you lose audio or video from every testimonial for an entire event. You will want to kill everyone." (p.14)

### Exception 4: Testimonial Fatigue
**Trigger:** Community tired of testimonial requests
**Action:** Space out competitions
**Rule:** "People tire of them, so you can't do them very often, like once or twice a year max" (p.21)

### Exception 5: No Physical Location
**Trigger:** Virtual/remote business
**Action:** Skip delivery moments, focus on call recording
**Note:** "If you're an in-person business you have an insane advantage over virtual or remote businesses. Every day is an in-person event." (p.12) - Virtual businesses use lifecycle ads from calls instead.

## Appendix A: The Six-Point Testimonial Script

```
1. INTERNAL STRUGGLE (Emotional Before)
   "What was rock bottom before doing this?"
   "What did you have to do that you hated?"
   "What things did you want to do that you couldn't?"

2. EXTERNAL STRUGGLE (Measurable Before)
   "What were your objective metrics?"

3. SKEPTICISM (Objection Handling)
   "What were your main concerns about buying/clicking/joining?"

4. OVERCOME (Decision Trigger)
   "What made you do it anyways?"

5. EXTERNAL VICTORY (Measurable After)
   "What are your objective metrics now?"

6. INTERNAL VICTORY (Emotional After)
   "What's been the best moment since?"
   "What can you do now you couldn't do before?"
   "What things did you hate doing you don't have to do anymore?"
```
